LATEST FANTASY BASEBALL NEWS

RotoWire Update Recommendation
Domonic Brown (OF)  PHI  3/18/2012
Brown (neck) took 25-30 swings in the batting cage Sunday, the Phillies' official site reports. Brown is dealing with a stiff neck that may have been caused by his napping on a team bus during a road trip. The injury appears to be minor.
Joakim Soria (P)  KC  3/18/2012
Soria left Sunday's game with a sore elbow, the KC Star reports. The Royals plan to evaluate Soria's status in the next couple of days.
David Herndon (P)  PHI  3/18/2012
Herndon worked 1.2 innings Sunday, allowing one run on two hits in the Phillies' 10-2 loss to the Blue Jays. Herndon has a 2.57 ERA in five outings this spring. He's a leading candidate for a spot in the Phillies' pen. "I'm going to go out there and pitch the best I can," Herndon told the team's official site. "They're going to make the decision."
Placido Polanco (3B)  PHI  3/18/2012
The Phillies expect Polanco (hand) to be sidelined for a few days, the team's official site reports. Polanco suffered a sprain when he dove back into first base during Saturday's game. "It's a little stiff today, there's a little swelling, but it's not a big thing," Polanco explained. "They told me to take it easy. I jammed it and it's day to day. We've got to see how I feel every day."
Jose Contreras (P)  PHI  3/18/2012
Contreras (elbow) made his first Grapefruit League appearance Sunday. Contreras, who is working his way back from elbow surgery, struggled in his outing. He faced five batters and recorded just one out.
Laynce Nix (OF)  PHI  3/18/2012
Nix (ribs) played right field in a minor league game Sunday, the Philadelphia Inquirer reports. Nix missed the last six Grapefruit League games sore ribs. He expects to be back in action Monday. "I think I'm good enough to go," Nix said. "Hopefully that's the case."
Jim Thome (1B)  PHI  3/18/2012
Thome played first base Sunday for the second day in a row, the Philadelphia Inquirer reports. "Two days in a row, I could definitely feel it," Thome said. "It's all good. I wanted to do that." Thome will continue to see time at first this spring as long as his body allows it. The Phillies hope to have him make around 20 starts at first base this season.
Tim Beckham (SS)  TB - AAA  3/18/2012
Beckham was optioned to the minors on Sunday, The Tampa Times reports. This doesn't come as a surprise as the 21-year-old was not expected to make the team. Beckham will man shortstop at Triple-A Durham and could see time later this season if Sean Rodriguez and Reid Brignac struggle.
Carlos Zambrano (P)  MIA  3/18/2012
Zambrano struck out seven and allowed one run over four innings during Sunday's Grapefruit League game against the Cardinals, the Marlins' official site reports. "I guess I went back to being a strikeout pitcher," Zambrano said. "Not too bad. I'll take [it]. I feel everything is there, the way I want it." With Big Z seemingly in a better place this season, he has serious sleeper value.
Zach Braddock (P)  MIL  3/18/2012
Braddock did not dress with other players for Sunday's game and Brewers GM Gord Ash said he couldn't comment on the situation, Tom Haudricourt of the Milwaukee Journal Sentinel reports. Braddock dealt with sleep-related issues last year and it caused him to miss time. He said earlier in camp that he had gotten over it, and it's uncertain if his absence has anything to do with that problem.
Jordan Schafer (OF)  HOU  3/18/2012
Schafer felt something in his wrist pop when he attempted a diving catch Sunday, but said the x-rays looked good, Brian McTaggart of MLB.com reports. "Hopefully I just sprained it and it will be OK," Schafer said. There doesn't appear to be much cause for concern here.
Corey Kluber (P)  CLE - AAA  3/18/2012
Kluber was assigned to minor league camp Sunday, Nick Camino of Newsradio WTAM 1100 reports. Kluber was one of five players sent to the minors Sunday. The others were Ezequiel Carrera, Juan Diaz, Hector Ambriz and Michel Hernandez.
Bryce Harper (OF)  WAS - AAA  3/18/2012
Harper struck out four times during Sunday's Grapefruit League game against the Tigers, William Ladson of MLB.com reports. Harper was optioned to Triple-A on Sunday, and while he's still a work-in-progress, Harper is a 19-year-old raw talent who could make it to the big show at some point this year.
Patrick Arlis (C)  MIL - AAA  3/18/2012
Arlis was returned to minor-league camp Sunday, Brewers' Media Relations Director Mike Vassallo reports. Arlis only got 11 plate appearances last season (Triple-A). In 2010, he batted .180/.328/.220 in 19 Triple-A games, so it's unlikely he provides fantasy value this season.
Jeff Bianchi (SS)  MIL - AA  3/18/2012
Bianchi was outrighted to Double-A Huntsville on Sunday, Brewers' Media Relations Director Mike Vassallo reports. Bianchi batted .259/.320/.333 last year in Double-A, and it would might take several injuries to the Brewers' middle infield for Bianchi to be called up this year.
Eric Hurley (P)  ANA  3/18/2012
Hurley was reassigned to minor league camp Sunday, Angels' play-by-play announcer Victor Rojas reports. The 26-year-old pitched 84.1 Triple-A innings last year, going 8-3 with a 5.55 ERA. There's a chance he cracks the back end of the rotation at some point in 2012.
Jarrod Saltalamacchia (C)  BOS  3/18/2012
Saltalamacchia (hip) played five innings Sunday in his return, Scott Lauber of the Boston Herald reports. Saltalamacchia, who has been dealing with hip bursitis, reportedly did not suffer any setbacks during his outing. He said of his condition, "It happens in old people and catchers apparently."
Bryce Harper (OF)  WAS - AAA  3/18/2012
Harper has been optioned to Triple-A, Adam Kilgore of the Washington Post reports. Some will be disappointed the 19-year-old didn't make the Opening Day roster. Still, the fact that the Nationals are confident enough to expose Harper to Triple-A after just 37 games at Double-A is telling. At 19 years old, Harper will be the youngest player at the level. If he excels there, it's entirely possible we see him in Washington before the season ends.
Jeff Francis (P)  CIN  3/18/2012
Francis is still in the running to be the Reds' fifth starter, XM's Jim Bowden reports. Performance aside, Francis is up against it a little bit. Homer Bailey is out of options, and Francis is in camp on a minor league deal. We're still more than a little wary of his skill set working in the Great American Ballpark.
Yorvit Torrealba (C)  TEX  3/18/2012
Torrealba was scratched from Sunday's game with lower back stiffness, T.R. Sullivan of MLB.com reports. Torrealba batted .273/.306/.399 with seven home runs and 37 RBI a season ago (113 games). Mike Napoli figures to get the vast majority of starts at catcher, but Torrealba will be in the mix occasionally. The extent of this injury is uncertain.
Ryan Tucker (P)  LA  3/18/2012
Tucker was reassigned Sunday, Ken Gurnick of MLB.com reports. Tucker only threw five innings of major league ball last season and posted a 7.20 ERA. In 2008 (his only other time in the majors), he put up an 8.27 ERA over 37 innings. In Triple-A last year, he posted a 5.40 ERA through 68.1 innings, and it looks like he'll have to work on his game a bit before he makes it to the big show again.
Brent Morel (3B)  CHI-A  3/18/2012
Morel (back) returned to the lineup Sunday, Scott Merkin of CBS reports. Morel was dealing with back stiffness but it doesn't appear to be a major issue. He remains the apparent starter at third base for the White Sox this season.
Trevor Plouffe (SS)  MIN  3/18/2012
Plouffe strained his right hamstring Sunday and will not play until at least Wednesday, the Minneapolis Star Tribune reports. Plouffe is hitting .276 (8-for-29) this spring. He's likely to win a utility role and this injury probably won't hurt his chances. He could have a larger platoon role with Ben Revere in left field, but it's too early in the spring to size up that scenario.
